Mango Sago

Mango Sago is a popular Cantonese dessert that has become popularized across Southeast and East Asian Countries. This refreshing dessert is typically enjoyed on hot summer days. Filled with mango, sago, coconut cream, and condensed milk, this chilled dessert is easy to make and great for a large crowd.

Ingredients

Yield: 8 servings

Total Time: 2.5 hours

  • 2/3 cup sago (tapioca pearls)
  • 10 cup water
  • 13.5 oz coconut milk
  • 4 ripe mangoes
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 6 tbsp condensed milk

Optional Toppings

  • 3/4 cup coconut milk
  • Mango cubes
  • Shredded coconut

Instructions

  1. Bring a cup with 10 cups of water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Add sago after water reaches a boil.
  3. Lower the heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes until sago is translucent.
  4. Drain and rinse sago under ice cold water, set aside.
  5. Peel and cut mangoes into 1 inch cubes
  6. Place coconut milk, condensed milk, vanilla extra in a medium sized saucepan.
  7. Bring the coconut milk mixture to a boil over medium-high heat, remove once boiling.
  8. Remove from heat and mix in sago until well incorporated..
  9. Chill in fridge for a minimum of 2 hours
  10. Remove from fridge and top with mangoes.
  11. Optional: Add coconut milk, mango cubes, and or shredded coconut for garnish.
